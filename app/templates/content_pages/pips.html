What are Pips?

In Forex, a pip is an acronym for “percentage in point” which is used to express the change in value of a currency pair. A pip is the smallest price movement that an exchange rate can make. A pip is usually the last decimal place of a price quote. Most currency pairs are quoted to the fourth decimal place. For example, if the EUR/USD is quoted at 1.2000 and price changes to 1.20001, that would mean an increase of 1 pip. Likewise, if the price changed to 1.1999, that would mean a decrease of 1 pip. Japanese Yen pairs are an exception, as they are quoted to the second decimal place. Pips are used by traders to calculate the profit or loss on a trade.
