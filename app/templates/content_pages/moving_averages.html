Moving Averages

The moving average (MA) is a simple technical analysis tool that smooths out price data by creating a constantly updated average price. The average is taken over a specific period of time, like 10 days, 20 minutes, 30 weeks or any time period the trader chooses. There are advantages to using a moving average in your trading, as well as options on what type of moving average to use.

Why moving averages are important?

On a price curve, a moving average will help reduce the volume of "noise." To get a general understanding of the way the market is going, look at the direction of the moving average. If it's angled up, the price is rising overall; if it's angled down, the price is falling overall; and if it's going horizontally, the price is most likely in a range.

Dynamic Support and Resistance

A moving average can also serve as resistance or support. A 50-day, 100-day, or 200-day moving average will serve as a support level in an uptrend, as seen in the chart below. Since the average serves as a floor (support), the price rises when it hits it. A moving average will serve as resistance in a downtrend; like a ceiling, the price reaches the plateau and then begins to fall again.

Moving averages aren't necessarily respected by the price. If the price is over a moving average, the direction is generally upward. The trend is down if the price is below a moving average. Moving averages, on the other hand, may have varying lengths (which will be addressed shortly), so one MA may mean an uptrend while another indicates a downtrend.
