Buying and Selling Currency Pairs

The simultaneous purchase of one currency and the sale of another is known as forex trade. Currencies are bought and sold in pairs by a broker or dealer. The value of a currency is expressed in comparison to another currency. The euro and the US dollar (EUR/USD) are two examples, as are the British pound and the Japanese yen (GBP/JPY). You buy and sell currency pairs while you deal in the forex market.

The relative price of two currencies from two separate countries is known as an exchange rate. Exchange rates fluctuate depending on which currency is more powerful at any given time.

There are three types of currency Pairs: the majors, the crosses and the exotics.

The US dollar is still included in the major currency pairs. The US dollar is not used in cross-currency pairs. Crosses involving any of the big currencies are called "minors." One major currency and one currency from a developing market make up exotic currency pairs (EM).

Major Currency Pairs

The following currency pairs are known as the "majors." They are the most often sold pairs and they all have the US dollar (USD) on one hand. Price swings more often with the majors than with crosses and exotics, providing more trade options.

The majors are the world's most liquid markets. It is calculated in forex based on the number of active traders buying and selling a particular currency pair, as well as the amount exchanged. The higher the liquidity of something, the more often it is sold. The EUR/USD currency pair, for example, is traded with more individuals and in higher amounts than the AUD/USD currency pair.

The Exotic Currency Pairs

One main currency is combined with the currency of a developing market, such as Brazil, Mexico, Chile, Turkey, or Hungary, to form exotic currency pairs.
