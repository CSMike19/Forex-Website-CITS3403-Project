What are lots?

A lot is a way to effectively and efficiently communicate standardized quantities of currency transactions. This is used widely and is preferred by traders as compared to using a specific currency when talking about trades. This is mainly because when you place market orders, they are placed in sizes known as lots.

Lots can come in many different sizes. The main ones are:

  • Standard Lot
  • Mini Lot
  • Micro Lot
  • Nano Lot

The standard lot is the equivalent of 100,000 units of any given currency. An example of this is that 1 standard lot of EUR/USD is equal to €100,000.

The mini lot, also known as the 0.1 lot is 10,000 units of any given currency. The micro lot, also known as the 0.01 lot is 1,000 units of any given currency.The nano lot, also known as the 0.001 lot is 100 units of any given currency.

Choosing Your Lot Size

Choosing the right lot size to trade with will depend very much on the size of your account and how you manage your risk.

For example, say you had an account containing $100 USD. Say you choose to trade with a standard lot with the EUR/USD pair. Since the value per pip is $10 USD, this means if the price moves 10 pips against you, your account will be blown! However, if you chose a smaller lot size, your account will have much more flexibility and can handle 10 pip drawdowns. This option is much more sustainable for your account. As your account becomes bigger and you become more confident in your trading plan, you can start to increase the lot size you trade with.
